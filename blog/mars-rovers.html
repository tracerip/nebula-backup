The Mars Rovers

Humanity has been exploring Mars robotically for decades. While orbiters provide a global view, rovers are the geologists on the ground, analyzing rocks and soil to understand the history of water on the Red Planet.

The Roster

  • Sojourner (1997)
    Part of the Pathfinder mission, this microwave-sized rover was the first wheeled vehicle to rove on another planet. It operated for 83 days.
  • Spirit & Opportunity (2004)
    The twin geologists. Spirit operated until 2010. Opportunity, designed for a 90-day mission, incredibly kept working until 2018, covering a marathon distance.
  • Curiosity (2012)
    A car-sized rover powered by a nuclear generator. It landed in Gale Crater to determine if Mars was ever habitable for microbial life (Answer: Yes). It is still active today.
  • Perseverance (2021)
    Based on Curiosity's design but with different instruments. Its primary mission is to seek signs of ancient life and collect samples for future return to Earth. It also carried the Ingenuity helicopter.
